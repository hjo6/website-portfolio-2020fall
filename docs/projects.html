PittCoffee

For my Capstone Project for Computer Science, I took a Team Project Design and Implementation class where I was assigned a project, a team to work on the project with, and a project sponsor/customer. For our project, my team developed a website/application called PittCoffee. Our application interacts with the user through conversation to order items from a coffee shop. We use artifical intelligence and natural language generation to be able to understand the user and respond in a natural manner. Our application is unique in that it is multimodal based on the user's context and needs; there is a text module so that the user can simply text the application, a speech module so that they can talk in real time to the application, and a touch module to edit the order. The scope of the project ended up being larger than we could complete in a single semester, so to fully realize our vision, we aim to continue collaborating with our project sponser in the future to develop a more mature natural language understanding and generation system. Our big picture goal is to collaborate on a research paper with graduate level students within the next few months.

My biggest contribution to the project would be in designing and implementing the website component. Originally, we had developed an application for Android devices; we switched to a website so that gathering data would be easier (as most of our developers had iOS devices). So around midway through the semester, I was tasked with designing the website. I used all of the knowledge I learned about webdesign from my Composing Digital Media class to undertake the creation of the website. My first draft was much more rough than the above design, but eventually I would rework the website into the above using further knowledge gained from my CDM class. I used div containers to construct all of the website, but containerizing the chat messages and getting them to display properly was a task that CDM had not completely prepared me for. I overcame the issue by using the sleuthing skills acquired both from my CDM website portfolio functionality, but also from previous CS courses in order to figure out how to implement the message system.

Other than the website design and some of the functionality, I also was tasked with developing the backend for the Android application. This entailed setting up a Flask server to host the application locally for testing, migrating the functions from the frontend to the backend to maintain separation of UI and functionality, and getting the basic responses from the backend to the frontend. This was all a lot of work, though it helped build the foundations needed for classes in the same semester (such as Cloud Computing and even Composing Digital Media).

I've provided images below of the HTML and CSS that I coded for the website part of this project. Our GitHub repository for the project is private, so I figured that this was a good way to showcase my work for the project.

Feature Extraction Using Harris Corner Detection

This program was the third homework assignment for my Intro to Computer Vision course. The homework assignment was to implement feature extraction using the Harris corner detector discussed in class. The images above show the detected keypoints (represented by the red circles) from 3 images I used to test the program. The size of the red circle corresponds to the cornerness score that the keypoint received: a larger red circle meant that a keypoint had a high R score (meaning that the feature detected is likely a distinct corner), whereas a smaller red circle meant that the keypoint had a lower R score (the feature detected is likely a corner, but less distinct). Shiver by Your Average Misfits

Where Did the Time Go? by Your Average Misfits

Where Did the Time Go? No, really, where did it go? This is one of four songs that I recorded with my high school band, Your Average Misfits. I was lead singer in the band, so all of the vocals are yours truly! This song was written around the same time as Shiver, but came maybe a few hours after the arrangement of Shiver. The Fall by Your Average Misfits

The Fall is one of four songs that I recorded with my high school band, Your Average Misfits. I was lead singer in the band, so all of the vocals are yours truly! This song is about finding the optimism in dark times. The subject of the song is the self; I would describe it as an introspective piece, looking at the self-destructive habits we have and trying to recognize and reconcile with them. This song went through an interesting songwriting cycle. We brought it into the studio for the producer to hear, and they wanted to change it from pop-rock to a much more punk-y sound. Originally, Brian would play piano during the verses, and guitar for the chorus, and I would play lead guitar in the verses. What we had written was a nearly seven minute song, which we ended up cutting down to about 3 minutes. The distorted, siren sounding keyboard (it sounds like a guitar, or maybe even a submarine SONAR) is actually a remnant from the original, as we tried to fit the piano in, but it did not blend well in the mix.

Toughts Don't Show by Your Average Misfits

Thoughts Don't Show is one of four songs that I recorded with my high school band, Your Average Misfits. I was lead singer in the band, so all of the vocals are yours truly! This song was basically written in the studio as we were recording. I remember vividly playing in the studio with the full band, and having the lyrics pulled up in my Notebook app on my phone. This song is dear to me because of that process. I think this was the closest we ever got to being a legitimate "recording" band. We recorded the different parts as we were writing, and then once we had a "whole" product, we recorded it in full. It was an interesting experience, and a process I still use when writing songs today. The song itself deals with struggling to find the words to explain the way you feel, and feeling misunderstood because of this struggle. It has taken me years to get to a level of communication where I can articulate exactly what I'm feeling and thinking. Listening again years later has made me realize that this song, and really this whole EP, are heavily influenced by naivete and innocence.
